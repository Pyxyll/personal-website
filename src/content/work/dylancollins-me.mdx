## The brief, to myself

The previous version had a Laravel backend, a Postgres database, a Next.js
frontend, and a React Native companion app, all to publish a post every couple
of weeks. This time the rule was: stop overbuilding.

## What it is

One static Astro site. Posts and projects live as MDX files, so publishing is
a commit and a push. A single dithered shader sits behind the home wordmark;
everything else is type and hairlines. The contact form posts to a tiny
Cloudflare Worker that calls Resend server-side, which keeps the site fully
static with no backend to babysit. It deploys to my own VPS through Coolify.

## What I kept

The pink accent, the monospace details, and the idea that a personal site can
be a little weird without being a maintenance burden.
