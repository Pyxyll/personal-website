## Why it exists

COSMIC didn't have a working colour picker. `hyprpicker` won't run because
`cosmic-comp` doesn't expose `zwlr_screencopy_v1`, and the desktop portal's
own `PickColor` handler is still a `// XXX implement` stub. So I built one.

## What it is

A PowerToys-style picker in three parts that all share one history file:

- **`cosmic-color-pickerd`** — a headless daemon that owns the IPC socket, runs
  the screen overlay on demand, and persists history. Autostarts at login.
- **`cosmic-color-picker`** — the GUI: a hero swatch, HEX/RGB/HSL/HSV readouts,
  a history strip, and a settings page with a click-to-record shortcut binder.
- **`cosmic-applet-color-picker`** — a panel applet with a pick button and a
  strip of recent colours.

The hotkey, the applet, and the GUI's pick button all funnel into the same
daemon, so every pick lands in shared history no matter where it came from.

It'll be obsolete the day System76 implements `PickColor` properly. Until then,
it's the stopgap I use every day.
